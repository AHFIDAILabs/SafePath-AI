Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of this tool?

This tool is designed to assist social workers, NGOs, and support organizations by providing an AI-driven risk assessment for Gender-Based Violence (GBV). It helps prioritize cases and allocate resources more effectively by identifying individuals who may be at high risk.

Important: While the model performs exceptionally well on historical data, no predictive model is perfect. There is always a risk of false positives (predicting high risk when the individual is not at high risk) and false negatives (predicting low risk when the individual is at high risk). Always use the tool as a supplement to, not a replacement for, professional judgment.

Note: This tool is intended to support, not replace, the expertise and judgment of trained professionals. Always consider the broader context of each case.

How accurate is the prediction model?

The underlying Gradient Boosting model was trained on a large dataset and achieved 99.6% accuracy and 99.5% sensitivity on the test set. Sensitivity is the most critical metric, as it measures the model's ability to correctly identify true high-risk cases, meaning it has a very low rate of missing vulnerable individuals.

Important: While the model performs exceptionally well on historical data, no predictive model is perfect. There is always a risk of false positives (predicting high risk when the individual is not at high risk) and false negatives (predicting low risk when the individual is at high risk). Always use the tool as a supplement to, not a replacement for, professional judgment.

What data is the model based on?

The model was trained on anonymized historical case data, including socio-economic indicators, demographic information, and social support network factors. No personally identifiable information (PII) is used or stored by this application.

Important: The model's predictions are based on patterns learned from the training data and may not capture all nuances of individual cases. Always use professional judgment in conjunction with the tool's output.

How should I interpret the results?

The tool provides a "High Risk" or "Low Risk" prediction, a probability score, and a confidence level. More importantly, it shows the Key Risk and Protective Factors that influenced the prediction. This explanation is crucial for understanding the "why" behind the result and can guide intervention strategies. The generated summary provides a quick, easy-to-understand narrative.

We encourage users to review these factors carefully and consider them in the context of their knowledge of the individual case.

Note: The model is not infallible. It may produce false positives (predicting high risk when the individual is not at high risk) and false negatives (predicting low risk when the individual is at high risk). Always use the tool as a supplement to, not a replacement for, professional judgment.

Is this tool a replacement for professional judgment?

Absolutely not. This is a decision-support tool, not a decision-making tool. The predictions should be used as one piece of information to supplement, not replace, the expertise and judgment of trained professionals.

How is data privacy handled?

The application is designed with privacy as a priority. The data you enter into the form is sent to our secure backend for real-time prediction and is not stored or logged. The entire process is ephemeral.
